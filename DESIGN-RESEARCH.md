# Design research: Overseas Virtual Assistant

## Search intent

- Query: `overseas virtual assistant services`
- Supporting query: `hire a virtual assistant Philippines services`
- Buyer intent: compare managed overseas assistants, understand what to delegate, and request help scoping a role without sorting through an open marketplace.

## Five direct organic competitors inspected

### 1. Wing Assistant

- URL: https://wingassistant.com/
- Strong points: crisp two-column hero, circular assistant portraits, one bright primary CTA, clear "managed, not marketplace" framing, and useful fit/not-fit panels.
- Weak points in the inspected desktop capture: the hero carries too many badges and proof claims; blue-purple gradients repeat across several sections; some small proof labels are hard to scan.

### 2. 20four7VA

- URL: https://20four7va.com/
- Strong points: unmistakable blue-and-orange identity, prominent phone and booking paths, service imagery, and a substantial legal/contact footer.
- Weak points in the inspected desktop capture: cookie and chat overlays compete with the hero, several calls to action have equal weight, and some media/testimonial areas appeared empty or unloaded.

### 3. Virtudesk

- URL: https://www.myvirtudesk.com/
- Strong points: spacious two-column hero, straightforward discovery-call CTA, consistent service cards, visible process steps, and a restrained white/blue/orange palette.
- Weak points in the inspected desktop capture: generic stock photography, small low-contrast body text, many competing actions, and a cookie banner that covered important first-screen content.

### 4. Remote CoWorker

- URL: https://remotecoworker.com/
- Strong points: distinctive cream, coral, and chocolate palette; bold editorial headline; friendly assistant portrait; floating workflow cards that make remote support feel tangible; clear primary and secondary actions.
- Weak points in the inspected desktop capture: the hero UI collage is crowded, some carousel content is clipped, and long-page sections appeared blank or delayed in the full-page capture.

### 5. VirtualStaff.ph

- URL: https://www.virtualstaff.ph/
- Strong points: simple centered hero, direct offer language, clean navigation, and a single dominant CTA.
- Weak points in the inspected desktop capture: little human imagery, no useful trust or role detail near the hero, faint decorative backgrounds, and very large blank areas where content appeared not to render.

## Chosen direction

Remote CoWorker is the strongest visual starting point. Its human-first hero and compact work cards explain the service before a visitor reads the whole page. The composition is useful, but its warm palette does not fit a global staffing brand.

The implementation keeps the human portrait and practical handoff notes, then moves in its own direction: a deep ocean hero, emerald and light-green accents, an off-center portrait framed like a global dispatch card, a time-zone handoff strip, and role cards built around tasks and owner guardrails. The page avoids competitor copy, logos, layouts, price claims, ratings, testimonials, client marks, and unverifiable numbers.

## Homepage plan

1. Use a short outcome-led hero: move recurring work out of the owner's day without losing control.
2. Show one relevant human portrait with small, useful handoff notes rather than fabricated profile credentials.
3. Put planning guidance near the first screen: scope first, a typical 7–21 day ramp, and a 5–10 task starter lane. These are explicitly labeled as planning guidance, not measured outcomes.
4. Present roles as a dispatch board with concrete tasks and a plain-language owner guardrail.
5. Explain time-zone choice through three practical coverage patterns.
6. Show the managed process as a concise four-step field guide.
7. Keep one conversion phrase throughout: `Request a staffing plan`.
8. Preserve the existing blog, contact, privacy, terms, cancellation, thank-you, sitemap, robots, metadata, schema, footer disclosure, and copyright.

## Originality and content constraints

- No competitor brand assets, copy, profile names, customer logos, ratings, awards, or proprietary illustrations.
- No public pricing or salary claims.
- No fabricated proof, client outcomes, testimonials, or employer badges.
- No references to the old shared template or another portfolio brand.
- Marketing copy uses plain constructions, varied sentence rhythm, and specific task language. It avoids inflated claims, vague authority, formulaic threes, excessive em dashes, and generic positive conclusions.
